🏆 Academic Design Project
Stair-Climbing Wheelchair — Design & Manufacturing

A fully functional stair-climbing wheelchair engineered to restore independent mobility for physically challenged individuals on staircases and uneven terrain — surfaces that conventional wheelchairs cannot navigate. The project covered the full product development cycle from concept ideation and mechanical design to fabrication, assembly, and performance testing.

⚙ Core Concept — Penta Wheel (Pentagonal Wheel) Mechanism
The wheelchair uses a Penta Wheel (pentagonal / five-spoke star wheel) arrangement in place of conventional circular wheels. Five small wheels are mounted radially at equal angular spacing (72° apart) around a central hub, forming a pentagonal profile. As the assembly rotates, successive wheels contact and step up stair edges — delivering a rolling-over-steps action with a near-smooth ride and minimal jerk. The geometry is optimised so at least one wheel is always in flat contact with a step surface, ensuring continuous traction and stability throughout the climb.
  • Concept: Pentagonal wheel arrangement — 5 radially mounted sub-wheels at 72° intervals, enabling step-over-step climbing without lifting the frame.
  • Design scope: Full mechanical design including wheel hub assembly, frame geometry, seat ergonomics, and load-bearing structure modelled in CAD.
  • Manufacturing: Fabricated using mild steel tube frame, welded joints, and machined wheel hubs; assembly and fit-out completed in-house.
  • Performance: Successfully tested on standard staircase geometry; demonstrated stable ascent and descent with minimal jerk transfer to the occupant.
  • Impact: Addresses a critical mobility gap for wheelchair users in buildings lacking ramp or elevator access — particularly relevant in older Indian infrastructure.
  • Analysis: Included static load analysis, centre-of-gravity calculations, and stability assessment across stair-climbing phases.
